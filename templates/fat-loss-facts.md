# Losing Fat Faster: Key Facts

## Combined Effect of All 4 Strategies

- Extra fat loss per week: ~1.2 lbs
- Average person on a typical diet loses only ~0.5 lbs/week
- Strategies can be used individually or combined

---

## Strategy 1: Reduce Fat Intake

- Protein and carbs: 4 calories per gram
- Fat: 9 calories per gram — more than double
- Minimum daily fat intake for health: 35–50 g/day — do not go below this
- Action: cut your usual fat sources in half (butter, cheese, avocado, fatty meats)
- Cutting just one fat source in half + one protein swap saves up to 250 calories/day
- Result: up to 0.5 lbs of extra fat loss per week

**High-fat protein sources to limit to once per day:** fatty cuts of meat, pork sausage, spam, processed meats

**For all other meals:** use lean, pure protein sources

**Eating out:** ask for half the usual portion of high-fat toppings (e.g. guac, cheese)

---

## Strategy 2: Walking (NEAT)

**Why cardio is less effective than expected:**
- A highly active person burns up to 2,000 more calories/day from NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis) vs. a sedentary person
- Cardio sessions often reduce NEAT for the rest of the day — people move less afterward
- Cardio also increases hunger, causing people to eat back the calories burned

**Walking targets:**
- Daily step goal: 7,000–12,000 steps
- 30-minute walk = ~3,000 steps = 100–200 calories burned
- Adding a daily 30-minute walk = ~1 extra lb of fat lost per month (~0.25 lbs/week)

**Practical ways to hit the target:**
- Under-desk treadmill: 2 × 30 min sessions while doing low-focus work = ~6,000 steps
- Park further away
- Walk around the block during breaks

---

## Strategy 3: The Accidental Deficit

- Burning 250 calories from jogging takes ~25 minutes — it is far easier to simply not eat those calories
- Best applied 1–2 times per week max, preferably on rest days
- Doing it once a week increases average daily calorie deficit by ~100 calories = ~0.25 lbs of extra fat loss per week

**How to execute a low-calorie day:**
- During busy days: eat only quick grab-and-go protein sources + fruit (protein bars, beef jerky, Greek yogurt cups, apples, bananas, oranges)
- Evening meal: large portion of lean protein + baked/steamed vegetables or a big salad
- Go to bed before snacking
- Total daily calories on these days: 1,300–1,600 (a large deficit for most people)

**Warning:** do not do this more than 1–2 times per week — losing weight too quickly risks muscle loss

---

## Strategy 4: Foods That Do the Work

### Protein's Thermic Effect
- 20–30% of protein's calories are burned during digestion, absorption, and metabolism — more than double any other macronutrient
- Switching from a low-protein to a high-protein diet raises daily calorie burn by ~4–5%
- Equivalent to a 10-minute jog every day
- Result: ~1 extra lb of fat loss per month

**Daily protein target:**
- In lbs: bodyweight (lbs) × 0.8 = grams of protein/day (e.g. 200 lbs → 160 g/day)
- In kg: bodyweight (kg) × 1.8 = grams of protein/day

### Fiber and Resistant Starch
- Study: two groups ate identical calories (2,100/day). The whole-food group excreted 116 extra calories/day — despite eating the same total calories
- Whole foods are also significantly more filling

**Swap processed foods for whole foods:**

| Instead of | Choose |
|---|---|
| Cereal | Oats |
| White rice | Potatoes or beans |
| Chips | Popcorn |
| Juice | Whole fruit |
| White bread | Whole grain or legumes |

- Make fruits and vegetables with every meal non-negotiable
- Start small and build the habit gradually
